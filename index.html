Politeness Theory

Brown & Levinson (1987)

This site provides supplemental instructional materials for linguistics students doing pragmatic analyses using the Politeness Theory framework put forth by Brown and Levinson (1978, 1987). The materials are intended to be used in a "flipped classroom" model and are designed to help linguistic pragmatics students understand major concepts of this theory and perform analyses of texts using this theoretical framework. The theory is divided into modules. Each module contains a short video lecture and an interactive quiz. The sequence of the modules is: 1. Overview of the Theory 2. Positive Politeness 3. Negative Politeness and 4. Extended Analysis. The site was designed by Kyle Roden as part of a culminating experience project for a Masters degree in Teaching English to Speakers of Other Languages at Portland State University.
